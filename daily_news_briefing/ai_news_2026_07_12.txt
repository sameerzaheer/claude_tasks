🤖 AI & LLMs

Apple sued OpenAI in Northern California federal court on July 11, 2026, alleging trade secret theft and citing more than 400 former Apple employees now at OpenAI, framing the hiring as a coordinated campaign to extract confidential technology. (https://www.forbes.com/sites/madhulika-pathak/2026/07/09/openai-debuts-chatgpt-work-workplace-ai-agent-with-gpt-56/)

The U.S. Department of Health and Human Services announced on July 10, 2026 that it will deploy ChatGPT to analyze state audit reports and hunt for fraud and waste across roughly $2.1 trillion in annual Medicare and Medicaid spending, with findings able to trigger withholding of federal funds from non-compliant states — one of the largest government deployments of a commercial LLM to date. (https://www.buildfastwithai.com/blogs/ai-news-today-july-12-2026)

🏗️ AI in AEC, Construction & Engineering

A new "AI in Construction Report 2026" from Vancouver-based Scius Advisory (published around July 6, 2026) found 90% of Canadian construction leaders believe AI can improve productivity, yet only 9% currently use it day-to-day, with 39% still piloting tools and 35% just researching them. (https://canada.constructconnect.com/dcn/news/technology/2026/07/canadian-construction-leaders-see-ai-promise-but-adoption-remains-limited)

Y Combinator's Summer 2026 batch (unveiled around July 8, 2026) is flooding construction and proptech with AI back-office tools, including FlowManual (an all-in-one AI platform for construction's back office), Foreman (AI-generated takeoffs and estimates replacing spreadsheets and email), and Rudus (AI estimating for concrete contractors that cuts estimation time by 70%). (https://www.marketscale.com/industries/engineering-and-construction/ycs-summer-2026-cohort-floods-construction-and-proptech-with-ai-back-office-tools)
